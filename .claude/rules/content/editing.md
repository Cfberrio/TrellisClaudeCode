# Content & Editing — Rules

## Purpose
Guidelines for content production, editing, briefs, scripts, and creative output across all Trellis brands.

## Brief Standards
A usable brief includes: objective, audience, platform, core angle, structure, CTA, required assets or references, and editing notes when relevant.

## Script Standards
Preserve the real hook, message, and CTA. Do not water down direct-response intent.

## Brand-Specific Guidance
- **Trellis**: authority in systems, AI workflows, operational leverage, founder thinking, automation, execution discipline.
- **CTS**: aesthetic, appetite-driven, sensory, warm, brand-consistent. Never sounds like a B2B agency.
- **DR**: energetic, clear, trustworthy, conversion-aware. Parents need clarity, cold audiences need specifics, recruiting needs opportunity clarity early.
- **OEV / RV**: speed, trust, logistics clarity, booking movement. Clean and unambiguous.

## CTA Rules
Make CTAs intentional. Avoid soft, generic endings. Favor next-step clarity.

## Content-to-Action
Assume cold audiences need clarity fast. One message per asset. Reduce vagueness. Increase descriptive specificity when conversion matters.
